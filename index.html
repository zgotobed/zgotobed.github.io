About

I am a PhD candidate in Physics at Colorado State University working in precision measurement with trapped ytterbium ions. My research focuses on precision spectroscopy, optical clock development, and numerical simulations of novel clocking schemes to test fundamental symmetries.

Research Interests

  • Precision spectroscopy of trapped ions (I've worked with Ytterbium 173 and Sr 87 ions)
  • Optical clocks and frequency metrology
  • Tests of Lorentz symmetry
  • Monte Carlo and stochastic simulations
  • Trapped-ion quantum computing

Projects

PyOpticL

Open-source Python library for modular optical circuit design and code-to-CAD workflows. Includes beam-path simulation, dynamic routing, and 3D-printable optical baseplates.

ARTIQ Experiment Framework

Class-based framework for trapped-ion quantum computing experiments, including Rabi flops, spectroscopy, state preparation and measurement, and optical clocking.

Optical Clock Simulation Suite

Python and C++ simulation framework for testing novel optical clocking methods and optimizing experimental protocols.
